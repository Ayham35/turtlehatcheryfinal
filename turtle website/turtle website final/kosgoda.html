Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, also known as the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project, is a popular tourist attraction and a turtle conservation center located in Kosgoda, Sri Lanka. The hatchery's primary focus is on protecting sea turtles and their eggs, as well as educating the public about the importance of conservation efforts.

The main objective of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is to increase the survival rate of sea turtles and contribute to their overall conservation. The center works closely with local communities, fishermen, and tourists to raise awareness about the threats faced by sea turtles and the need for their protection.

Things to do in the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

One of the primary activities at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is the collection and protection of sea turtle eggs. Local villagers and fishermen who encounter turtle nests along the coast bring the eggs to the hatchery to ensure their safety. The staff carefully incubates the eggs in a controlled environment to increase the chances of hatching.

Visitors to the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery have the opportunity to witness the release of the baby turtles into the sea. This experience is not only educational but also serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of conservation efforts and the impact that individuals can have on protecting these endangered creatures.
