Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular conservation project located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to the protection and preservation of sea turtles, which are an endangered species.

The main objective of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is to collect sea turtle eggs laid on the beaches and provide a safe environment for their incubation. The hatchery team carefully monitors the nesting sites along the coast, and when they discover a nest, they collect the eggs to protect them from predators and human activities. The eggs are then transferred to the hatchery's incubation area.

Things to do in Hikkaduwa


There are many things to do to enhance your experience at the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery. Some of which are:

  1. Learn about sea turtle conservation: Take the opportunity to learn about the various sea turtle species found in Sri Lanka and the conservation efforts undertaken by the hatchery.
  2. Witness the nesting process: Depending on the time of year, you may have the chance to witness a sea turtle nesting or egg-laying event.
  3. See baby turtles: The hatchery often has tanks or enclosures where baby turtles are kept before their release.
  4. Participate in turtle releases: If you're fortunate, you might be able to participate in a turtle release event. The hatchery organizes these releases periodically, where visitors are invited to release baby turtles into the ocean
